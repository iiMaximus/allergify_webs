5 Surprising Foods with Hidden Allergens

Published on March 14, 2026 · 5 min read · Written by Allergify Team

Living with food allergies is a constant balancing act between enjoying food and staying safe. While most people know to avoid peanuts in a jar of nuts, many allergens are hidden in products you'd never suspect. In this guide, we dive deep into 5 common foods that might be putting you at risk.

1. Soy in Dark Chocolate

Many premium dark chocolate bars use soy lecithin as an emulsifier to create that smooth, melt-in-your-mouth texture. While some highly refined soy oil is safe for certain people, soy lecithin can trigger reactions in sensitive individuals. Always check the allergen statement, even on high-end brands.

2. Milk in Potato Chips

Believe it or not, even non-cheesy chips like Salt & Vinegar or BBQ flavor often use "Whey" or "Milk Powder" to help the seasoning stick to the chip. This is a classic example of why scanning every single product is vital for those with dairy allergies.

3. Shellfish in Worcestershire Sauce

Traditional Worcestershire sauce contains fermented anchovies. This is a major risk for anyone with a fish or shellfish allergy. It's often found in Caesars, steak marinades, and stews where you wouldn't expect a fish component.

4. Wheat in Soy Sauce

Most standard soy sauces are actually made with a significant amount of wheat as a fermentation base. If you're gluten-intolerant or allergic to wheat, look for Tamari or coconut aminos as a safe alternative.

5. Nuts in Pesto

Pesto is traditionally made with pine nuts, but many store-bought versions swap them for cashews, walnuts, or even peanuts as a cost-saving measure. Cross-contamination in manufacturing plants is also extremely high with pesto products.
